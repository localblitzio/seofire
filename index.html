  • Diagnose the Situation: Quickly assess the current SEO health of the site and pinpoint the major issues causing poor rankings, such as penalties, algorithm changes, or on-site problems.
  • Analyze Rankings and Penalties: Use SEO tools to identify rankings drops and determine if any penalties have been applied by search engines, especially Google.
  • Review On-Page SEO: Check for common on-page issues like title tags, meta descriptions, header tags, and keyword optimization.
  • Evaluate Content Quality: Ensure all content is high-quality, relevant, and provides value to the user, updating or removing subpar content.
  • Audit Backlink Profile: Carefully review the backlink profile for unnatural or low-quality links and develop a plan to disavow harmful links.
  • Address Technical Issues: Identify and fix technical SEO issues such as slow loading times, mobile-unfriendliness, and crawl errors.
  • Improve User Experience: Focus on enhancing the overall user experience on the site to reduce bounce rates and encourage engagement.
  • Implement Structured Data: Use schema markup to help search engines understand the context of the content and improve visibility in search results.
  • Develop a Content Strategy: Create a plan for new content that targets the right keywords and addresses the needs of the target audience.
  • Rebuild Trust with Search Engines: Through consistent and ethical SEO practices, aim to regain the trust of search engines over time.
  • Foster Social Engagement: Increase the site’s presence on social media platforms to drive traffic and improve the site’s authority.
  • Setup Performance Tracking: Install tracking tools such as Google Analytics and Google Search Console to monitor progress and make data-driven decisions.
  • Educate Key Stakeholders: Communicate the SEO plan and its importance to everyone involved with the site including managers, content creators, and developers.
  • Develop an Ongoing SEO Plan: Create an ongoing SEO strategy that includes regular content updates, backlink acquisition, and monitoring of the competitive landscape.
  • Maintain Open Communication: Keep the client informed about SEO progress and challenges throughout the process to manage expectations and ensure transparency.
